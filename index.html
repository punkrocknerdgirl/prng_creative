// the human behind this

I build systems from real work, not productivity fantasies.

Bookkeeper by trade, systems builder by compulsion. I've been inside enough small business messes — bad bank feeds, mangled A/R, payroll chaos, spreadsheets that made me question humanity — that I build tools to fix the exact problems I've already lived through.

Gen-X is officially old now. Hooray. We couldn't be more thrilled... I am fine that I am '"I watched The Empire Strikes Back" in the theater' many years old. It means I've spent a lot of years in the fields I am building around. I was good at those things because I figured out how to make them easier. One defining trait of my generation is we could figure out a way to get X done, but still get down to the lake to smoke Y with M. Any trick we can pull to allow us more fuck-off-able hours, we will find a way.

I still write notes in a paper journal, on stickies - that I lose and spend 20 min on the floor looking for until both my knees and my back hurt and I need to lie down. But I also watched my dad build Frankenstein-worthy computers from random parts he found or bought or traded at some swap meet. Not pretty machines, but functional, and to me as a kid - pure fucking magic.

Come along with me on my journey to prove that old dogs can le.... you know, I've never liked that saying. Whatever. Come or don't. I'll be here fucking around with ones and zeros while you fuck around with being lame. Tell your mom I said hi. Peace.
